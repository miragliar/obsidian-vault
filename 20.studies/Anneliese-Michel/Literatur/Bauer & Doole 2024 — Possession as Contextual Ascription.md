# Bauer & Doole (2024) — Possession as Contextual Ascription (anti-essentialistisch)

> [!info] Literaturnotiz
> **Autor:in:** Bauer & Doole · **Jahr:** 2024
> **Kurztitel:** Possession as Contextual Ascription (anti-essentialistisch)

1

spirits/daeomons are sentient, invisible entities interacting with humanity. across world there have been and are beliefs that external power can enter into a human being. to cause suffer or communicate

there is not one single unifying idea of possession.. each culture has its own

this process occurs across social, cultural and religious contexts.

2

FRAMING POSSESSION

there is an extraordinary cultural and historical diversity of spirit possession and a complexity.

possession is not a phenomenon in itself, rather a meaning ascribed to the status or behavior of certain people. people can be regarded as possessed, if they describe themselves as possessed

3

or if they are considered to be possessed by others in a given social group

what counts as possessed can only be determiend within a given context. So it can be historically, socially, culturally, religiously or secularly be ascribed.

so we simply cannot "know"… previously we thought we can give it certain characteristic signs… nowadays we approach it as an activity constrained by cultural models

4

Western negative mindset -> comes from Christian churches. exorcism… also because Jesus did some exorcisms…

yet for the most part demon possession in Gospels is studied with interest in narrative-literary aspects… in any case there is little doubt that the historical Jesus was understood as an exorcist who cast out demons…

5

negative cultural view of possession has also influenced academic study of the topic… most Western academics focus on possessions that imply pain and torment… and this encouraged even more the negative associations… meaning not many scholars showed big interest in the topic

in recent years we have seen varioius disciplines, anthropology, psyhology, scoiology, religious studies, etc. showing growing interest in research on possession…

6

it becomes apparent that there is a need for the academic study of possession not only within but also across disciplines

DEFINING POSSESSION

it is a widespread and diverse phenomenon… there have been attempts at a broad definition e.g.

Crapanzano 1977: altered state of consciousness indigenously interpreted in terms of the influence of an alien spirit. possession as an idiom for articulating a certain range of experience.

7

Lambek 1989: critical! generalizations that attempt to smooth over the variability and complexity of the phenomenon instead of giving them a central place are not particularly useful… possession as performance . like a ritual that does not merely speak. a system of communication

Boddy 1994: phenomena that we call possession are part of daily experience not just dramatic rituals! they have to do with ones relationship to the world, selfhood and moral identity. it creatively resituates individuals in a profoundly alienating or confusing world

8

possession is a bout morality, kinship, ethnicity, history and social memory.

Johnson 2011: spirit possession is ownership or occupation of body by unseen agents emerged out of an analogical relation with material possession and lands

so we see: variety of approaches … that apply different methods…

Huskinson 2010: a useful universal account of spirit possession … is not possible, as there wil always be cases that do not conform the proposed typologies

9

possession must be treated on a case-by-case basis… even within a single society, they are not gonig to be identical to one another…

STUDYING POSSESSION

article talks about different studies from Egpyt, Dead Sea Scrolls, Apostle Thomas, Christian demonology,…

10

Jewish ideas of possession, Roman Catholig exorcist in Graz, modern-day Austria, South America, India
